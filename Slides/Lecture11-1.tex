\frame{
\frametitle{Short Writing Exercise}
\large
\begin{quote}
Many citizens do not vote. Many citizens do not pay attention to politics and thus know very little. Many citizens do not have opinions. Many citizens make up opinions when asked, meaning their views are unstable over-time. If many citizens fail to have coherent opinions (or opinions at all), then there is no such thing as an aggregate ``public opinion.'' It is a phantom. It does not exist.
\end{quote}
}

\frame{

	\end{itemize}
\item Come together for a debate
\end{itemize}
